Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most commonly asked questions about our diabetes detection system, which uses models like SVM, Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN).

This system uses machine learning models such as SVM, Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and KNN to detect diabetes based on user-provided health data, ensuring accurate and early diagnosis.

Enter your health data (such as age, glucose level, BMI, etc.) into the input fields. The system will analyze this data using the chosen machine learning model to determine the likelihood of diabetes.

We use Support Vector Machine (SVM), Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) models. Each has its unique approach to detecting diabetes with a high degree of accuracy.

Each model offers different advantages. SVM is highly accurate for complex datasets, Logistic Regression is easy to interpret, Random Forest provides robust predictions, and KNN is simple and effective for smaller datasets. You can experiment with different models to see which works best for your data.

The accuracy of predictions depends on the quality of the input data and the model used. Generally, all models used in this system provide high accuracy, but they are not a substitute for medical advice and should be used as part of a broader diagnosis process.
